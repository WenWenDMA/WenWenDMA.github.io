Artist Statement

Look, feel, try, and play: these are my main creative processes in making this portfolio. I enjoy finding the beauty of life through various media, and I see myself improving every day and exploring new challenges with excitement. During the process of creating each piece of work, I have learned new ways of creating and forming ideas. Focusing on every process, I explore shapes, patterns, and materials, and I am often surprised with the visual outcome. I am especially interested in using colors to express my ideas and convey emotions, as I find that color can work as a means to connect people to the world; color has its own character and can reveal one’s personality and dreams.

I also find that I am very good at using different media to express my creativity. As I delve deeper into digital media, I have discovered a world of fun creative images and videos, where one can play and, through play, inspire people.

When I create joy and meaning, sharing my art, and expressing myself, I feel free. I hope that I can use my talents to bring many ways of seeing this world to those who will see my work and demonstrate that we are all in this world together.

Birth Day


1280px*1024px

Autodesk Maya

March, 2018

This still-life image was inspired by my conversation with my parents on my birthday and a ceramic heart I made a few years ago. Ceramic making is a very interesting process, so I decided to make it into the virtual scene. The ceramic heart is fired in the kiln and covered with a smooth glossy glaze, but this heart has a deep pink grassy texture and cannot be touched. It embraces a newborn heart. This small heart is covered with sky blue and cloud white. This scene can express a person's mood, a family, or an environment. The mirror reflects their thoughts and reminds me of my original intentions, to welcome every new beginning of life with the inner peace and purity.

Clock #1


P5js

April, 2018

When I was creating this clock, I realized that one minute was actually much shorter than I thought. Rectangular bar on the image moves downwardly and widened with time. The number that represents the hour will move to the second hand. The color will change from dark to bright every hour, until the start of the next hour; the canvas will be completely white. In this sketch, I used transition commands, time commands, and map commands to depict clock changes. Click on the picture to see the time.

Clock #2


P5js

April, 2018

Based on clock #1, I use different colors and shapes to make more interesting visual changes. Colors will change with different times of the day, and graphics will change every minute. I especially chose fresh and bright colors to make it look childlike. Click on the image to enter the sketch, move the mouse, you will see a moving question mark face.

Ivory


1280px*1024px

Soft Pastels, Autodesk Maya, Adobe Photoshop

April, 2018

This work was inspired by a wildlife photographer's descriptions of his nine-year life in Serengeti National Park. Wild animals have never actively attacked humans or ferocious beasts that we have imagined. However, in recent years, Africa has encountered only two dangers. One is bad weather and the other is from poachers. Many tourists will buy ivory souvenirs in Africa. It looks like a small ivory pendant, but it is actually an elephant's life. He shared an example of a small elephant. Last September, the baby elephant's mother was killed by poachers, the elephant was sent to their rescue center, but it did not eat or drink for a week, and died of hunger strike. All people cried, guessing that the elephant must have seen the scene of its mother being killed by humans, causing indelible psychological damage to it. Therefore, it is particularly resistant to humans, preferring to die and not accepting human help. So the photographer's T-shirt says: "Only Elephant Should Wear Ivory."

Long Nails


1280px*1024px

3D Scanner, Autodesk Maya, Adobe Photoshop

April, 2018

This is a Prosthetic project I made in the 3D modeling class. I used a 3D scan of my body and modeled a prosthetic for myself.

Install nails... time flies so slowly. I know that each nail has a specific function, but I didn't have a chance to read the instructions. They grow very fast. I accidentally scratched my leg, my feet, my back. I want to take off my nails, but I don't remember how to do it. They have already entered my brain and penetrated my chest. I am not afraid at all. In fact, I did not feel any pain. This seems like a dream. When I wake up, everything will disappear.

Put a Song on Repeat


960px*540px

H.264, 25”

Autodesk Maya, Adobe Premiere

April, 2018

It is a song that I have repeatedly played while creating this 3D animation. This song is called “Nunchakus", Jay Chou’s work in 2001. The lyrics of this song have a special rhyme and combine Chinese elements. I put some of my previous photographic works and ancient Chinese characters on the texture of the boxes, with the dynamic movements and lens changes, the effect seems to be similar to the feeling I get from music.
